About Me

I am a web designer, front-end engineer and graduate of DIY (Do-It-Yourself) internet learning. I began to learn web development began in early 2015. Since, I have done any and everything to gain a breadth of knowledge in all things code. I started, like most, doing courses on CodeAcademy, went on to finish the front-end and full-stack certifications through FreeCodeCamp. The latter has proved to a huge resource and was afforded the opportunity to contribute to real-world non-profit projects as a result.

Javascript is my stack of choice for its rosbustness, and how it allows maximal efficiency, speed and rapid development. I've done web development for a few clients independently and my workflow is heavily reliant of HTML, CSS, Javascript/Jquery, MongoDB databases, and Node.js.

I have an interest in Reasearch & Development (R&D), mastering new and emerging technologies and look forward to my growth and maturation as a developer. Technology is only as good as the people creating it, and recognize the importance of staying abreast of evolving technology to solve the world's thoughest problems. When my head isn't buried in a laptop, my time is occupied with my four kids. A few of my favorite things in life are classic literature, Italian horror films, and stuffing my face with all the unique dining available here in Charlotte.

blackGirlsCode(2)

Starting BGC2 is one of the most ambitious initiatives I've taken to date. Like many women of color, Software Developers that look like them are hard to come by. Before learning to code, what I imagined a Software Engineer to be did not look like me and the thought to pursue a technology never even crossed my mind, and writing my first line of code was -- scary. What I realized, after a while is my knack for solving problems is much better served through code. blackGirlsCode(2) exists to connect and build a community of women of color in technology and close the gap in the digitial divide. It's a safe place for women of color to learn software development and grow in their careers. Our members come from different backgrounds, either self-taught or college educated, all with a unique story that has brought them together. The common thread that binds us is to seek likeness in another and a burning desire to create our very own versions of disruptive technology.

The vision of bGC(2) continually evolves as new members come for coffee && code, or when someone shares a stellar idea. If you are interested to learn more about what we have going on check us out on Meetup, or blackGirlsCode.github.io If you wish to contribute to any of our projects, find us on GitHub
